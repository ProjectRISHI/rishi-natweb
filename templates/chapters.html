We believe college students are key agents of change and have the power to take the right approach to create sustainable change.

UC Irvine has chosen to support the village of Elandaikudam, located in Ariyalur District, Tamil Nadu. With a population of 4958, majority of the population works in agriculture (rice and sugarcane). There is a shortage of toilets in the village - only 30% of families have toilets. Furthermore, the water in the village is contaminated with chemicals, causing villagers obtain water through nearby village.

UCI Project RISHI


Northwestern University

The Northwestern chapter of Project RISHI was founded in May 2011. Northwestern is working to support the village of Charnia, located in Haryana, India. The Charnian population mainly consists of farmers and laborers; the laborers are mainly employed in a brick manufacturing factory, and therefore, they suffer from a wide range of respiratory issues. In addition, anemia and pregnancy and maternal/child health issues are prevalent in Charnia.

projectrishinu


University of California, Davis

The University of California at Davis chapter was founded in 2009. This chapter works alongside the UCSD chapter to support the villages of Anadhwan, Somnath, and Hemelkasa, all located in Marashtra, India. These were founded by renowned philanthropist Baba Amte, who focused his efforts for the rehabilitation and empowerment of the under-privileged, those particularly plagued by leprosy. The chapter at UC Davis works with those same ideals with the hopes to continue and further his goals. The UCDavis Chapter's first trip was in the summer of 2010 along with UCSD. During the summer of 2013, UC Davis hopes to launch their second trip working with their goals of which include, water purification, a supply of basic first aid kits in all the villagers homes, as well as the construction of a computer lab where students can learn and grow to keep up with the modern world. The chapter at UC Davis continues their on campus efforts in social awareness to the problems facing third world countries such as India as well as fundraising projects.

Contact: ucdavis@projectrishi.org

Project RISHI @UCDavis


Cal Poly Pomona

The California State Polytechnic University, Pomona (Cal Poly Pomona) chapter was founded in Fall 2013. The chapter has officially adopted the villages of Jhundpur, Tanda, and Jagdishpur in the Sonepat District of Haryana following their first India trip in September 2014. During this trip, several disparities were observed, specifically those of health, education, and energy. The 3 villages in total have a population of 6,000 people with one sarpanch, located in the Tanda village. 90% of the population is considered to be the labor class with work mainly consisting of farming and construction. The main health issues present in this village include anemia, diarrhea, and malaria, which are addressed primarily in the dispensary located in Jhundpur.

Contact: cpp@projectrishi.org

UCSD Project RISHI


University of California, Riverside

UC Riverside currently works in Pariej, a rural village located near the district of Kheda in the state of Gujarat. In Pariej, people from different castes live together with harmony. Most people in the village belong to the Kshatriya caste and they are either farmers or laborers. In the village, there are five small schools for children (like kindergarten), one primary school (Kumar-Kanya School) which consists of grades 1-8, and one high school (Mithaivala High School) which has grades 9 and 10. Also, there is one primary healthcare center and one Dena Bank.
