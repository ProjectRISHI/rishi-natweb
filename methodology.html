Methodology


Our projects are based on a rigorous 5-Step-Methodology which ensures that all critical aspects of a project is executed effectively with the necessary oversight. This process helps us ensure that all projects are moving our mission forward.


  • Understand

    Project RISHI members cannot identify a problem in a village without first engaging and partnering with local villagers and partnering organizations who are physically present year-round. Only after understanding the perspectives, wants and needs of the local population who are directly impacted by the disparity can Project RISHI warrant further exploring the problem. Additionally, research on solutions to address this disparity should not begin until the identified problem and the specific conditions surrounding this disparity have been thoroughly understood. A few standard questions that can apply to all projects are included.

  • Research

    In order for Project RISHI to be successful, it needs to partner with organizations that have successfully addressed the identified issue. Project RISHI is typically present in the target area during the summer months, and students are balancing a university education along with other activities. Partnering with other organizations that have a local presence will address these issues and improve efficiency, outcome, and sustainability. Project RISHI students are not coming up with our solutions; we are not reinventing the wheel. Instead, we are following other more experienced, full-time professionals that have already worked on promising solutions.

  • Finance

    Project RISHI understands that all projects require financial backing. We strive to make our projects successful and therefore, we ensure that funding a project takes the forefront of our efforts towards the early stages prior to implementation. This way no project is hindered due to a lack of funds at point during the implementation stage.

  • Implement

    Planning ahead for the timeline of implementation will help ensure that no one particular aspect of the project is going to unduly delay the implementation of it. In addition, it will be important to consider the timeline as it will inform funding applications -- e.g., when does funding need to be available and for how long do we need it? It is also essential to clearly identify, at the outset, who is responsible for what, as this will help ensure that no important tasks fall through the cracks and that there is efficient distribution of responsibilities.

  • Evaluate

    Evaluating the efficacy of implemented solution is critical to assessing whether the solutions we implement address the demands and needs of our target population -- the main driver behind our efforts. Even though our solutions will have been well-vetted prior to implementation and have shown evidence for success in other projects, we cannot take for granted that they will be effective in our particular population, given the large number of variables that determine outcome of projects. Our goal is to transcend beyond just being an organization that identifies needs and raises money to address them -- we want to be sufficiently critical of our own work and make improvements where necessary.
